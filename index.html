My resume as a Latex document.

On this page you find some instructions on how to get your own profesionally typesetted resume in minutes, making use of the ScribTex web service.

Something...

Step One: instant gratification!

With in the text area below you can grab my resume all merged in one file.

Focus on the textarea (click it), select all (CTRL-A or CMD-A), copy to clipboard (CTRL-C or CMD-C).


Now go to ScribTex.com and hit the "Try it now" button. Click the main.tex file and replace the content with what you just copied.

Hit the "Compile" button to see it.

Now you could hit the back button and replace my content (bottom of the document) with your own content, while hitting "Compile" regularly to see if it works. But as you might not have a login yet you might loose you data. So better fork it on GitHub, and work from there to keep track of your modifications.

Step Two and Three: fork on GitHub and compile with XeLaTeX

As explained it is wise to keep your resume in GitHub, especially if you already expose your Linkedin profile to the world, you might ask well do it geeky. So go ahead and fork it on GitHub.

In oder to squeeze most out of the fonts used I prefer to use XeLaTeX, which ScribTeX does not support (yet). Please have a look at the project page to find our how to install it.
